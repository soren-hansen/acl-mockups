Three-Phase Construction Cleaning Protocol

Construction cleaning is a sequenced program. Each phase is timed to a construction milestone. Skipping or collapsing phases means re-doing work — at the CPM’s cost and on the CPM’s schedule.

Phase 1

Rough Clean

Executed before flooring installation

Removes large debris, trade waste, and construction materials. Prepares the substrate for flooring installation without contamination.

What happens: Debris removal, dust control, surface wipe-down of framing and structure, construction waste segregation for NYC-compliant disposal.

Flooring installed over a contaminated substrate fails adhesion. Dust left in HVAC returns circulates through the completed building. This phase is substrate preparation — not cosmetic.
Phase 2

Light Clean

Executed during punch-list completion

Deep cleaning of the building interior and exterior as construction concludes. Aligned to trade sequencing — we clean after trades complete zones, not before.

What happens: Window cleaning, surface cleaning of walls, ceilings, fixtures and cabinetry, floor cleaning per surface type, restroom sanitisation, exterior grounds.

Cleaning before trades finish means re-cleaning after. We coordinate on zone sequencing — work completed once, per zone, correctly.
Phase 3

Final Clean

Before owner walkthrough · Supervisor-verified

Detail cleaning of every surface. Supervisor-led zone verification against the owner’s expected standard.

What happens: Detailed surface cleaning throughout, glass cleaning, surface-specific floor care (VCT, vinyl, concrete, marble, hardwood), fixture and hardware cleaning, final debris removal, photo documentation by zone.

Supervisor verification: Zone-by-zone inspection before CPM walkthrough. Deficiencies corrected before sign-off. Timestamped photo log formatted for GC’s handover package.

Touch-up clean available after owner walkthrough for items identified during the walk. Same crew, same supervisor, same documentation.

Technical Credibility

Fine Construction Dust Fails Inspections. We Eliminate It.

Fine particulate dust is one of the most common causes of failed inspections and callbacks after a post-construction clean. It settles on surfaces after cleaning, circulates through HVAC systems, and accumulates in vents, sills, and edges that a standard wipe-down misses.

Our crews use HEPA-filtered vacuuming throughout every phase. Surfaces are cleaned in sequence — high to low, room by room — so dust disturbed during cleaning doesn’t re-contaminate completed areas. Vents, returns, window tracks, and door hardware are cleaned as part of the final phase standard. Not as extras.

HEPA-filtered vacuuming throughout all phases
Captures fine particles standard vacuums recirculate into the air
High-to-low sequenced cleaning per room
Dust falls once — surfaces are cleaned in sequence, not cleaned twice
Vents, returns, tracks, and hardware — standard
The accumulation points that show up on inspection. Not optional add-ons.

The issue: Dust disturbed during Phase 1 and 2 settles on finished surfaces if sequencing is wrong. Getting the order right — and using HEPA throughout — is what separates a first-pass result from a callback.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a post-construction cleaning company commercial-grade — and how is that different from residential?

Commercial construction cleaning runs in phases aligned to construction milestones — rough clean before flooring, light clean during punch-list, final clean before owner walkthrough. It requires supervisor-led quality verification, coordination with other trades, and documentation for the GC’s handover package. Residential cleaning companies offer a single “final clean” visit. They are not built for milestone-based sequencing, multi-trade coordination, or CO documentation.

What documentation does Advantage Cleaning provide for the GC’s close-out package?

Timestamped photo logs of completed zones, supervisor sign-off by area, waste disposal carrier records for NYC-compliant construction waste removal, and a completion report formatted for the GC’s handover package. Inspection records are available on demand.

How does Advantage Cleaning coordinate with the construction schedule?

We coordinate directly with the site superintendent and trade leads on zone sequencing. Phase 1 (rough clean) is scheduled before flooring installation. Phase 2 (light clean) runs concurrent with punch-list completion. Phase 3 (final clean) is scheduled after all construction work concludes. The CPM sets the milestone dates — we work to them.

What happens if the owner walkthrough identifies items after the final clean?

We provide a touch-up clean after the owner walkthrough — same crew, same supervisor, same documentation standard. Identified items are addressed and re-documented before final sign-off.

Can Advantage Cleaning handle large-scale commercial projects?

Yes. We completed post-construction cleaning for a 750,000+ sq ft commercial soundstage in Queens — full phase sequencing, supervisor-verified, documented handover. Crew size scales to project scope and deadline. The documentation and supervisor accountability standard does not change with scale.

How is construction waste disposal handled in New York City?

Commercial construction waste in New York City must be removed by licensed private carters with documented chain-of-custody. We manage compliant waste removal — carrier documentation is included in the project close-out package. This is not an optional add-on. It is built into every commercial construction cleaning engagement.

Most of our construction cleaning engagements are directly with general contractors and their project managers. Facility owners and property managers also engage us for post-renovation cleaning of occupied buildings. Either works — the process and documentation standard is the same.

Do you work across multiple project types — offices, schools, healthcare, retail?

Yes. We have completed post-construction cleaning across office buildings, charter schools, healthcare facilities, hotels, retail, and cultural institutions across New York City, New Jersey, and Connecticut. The three-phase protocol and documentation standard apply regardless of project type.

Floor Types We Service

Every Surface Type. Surface-Specific Protocol.

Each floor type requires different chemistry, equipment, and maintenance cycles. Using the wrong approach accelerates wear, voids warranties, and creates replacement costs far exceeding correct care.

VCT (Vinyl Composition Tile)

VCT is the most common commercial hard floor — and the most frequently damaged by incorrect stripping. The finish protects the tile itself. Strip it incorrectly, skip neutralisation, or under-coat the reapplication, and you’re accelerating the tile’s lifecycle toward replacement.

What we do: Full strip to bare tile — dwell time documented, neutralisation step verified, 4–6 finish coats, 30-min dry time between each.

You receive: Before/after photographs, dwell time log, coat-by-coat application record, supervisor sign-off. Inspection records available on demand.

Annual / Semi-annual · Green cleaning compliant

Carpet

Daily vacuuming only removes surface soil. Embedded particulates — the ones that cause premature wear — require hot-water extraction. Skipping scheduled deep cleans shortens carpet lifecycle and increases allergen load.

What we do: HEPA vacuuming + spot treatment daily. Deep clean via hot-water extraction with pre-spray, agitation, and neutralising rinse every 3–6 months.

You receive: Before/after photographs, product & dwell time log, moisture reading post-extraction, stain treatment record, warranty preservation documentation.

Quarterly · Green cleaning compliant

Marble & Natural Stone

Marble and natural stone are capital assets. Restoration via diamond progression defers replacement — often by decades. The risk is damage from incorrect products: acidic cleaners etch marble permanently. We assess condition before any work begins.

What we do: Diamond progression: grind → hone → densify → polish. Grit sequence logged at each stage. Densifier with documented cure period. Sealer with product spec logged. Stone-safe daily maintenance.

You receive: Condition photographs at each stage, grit progression log, sealer product specification, slip-resistance verification documentation.

Annual · Class A

Concrete (Warehouse & Industrial)

Warehouse and industrial concrete floors accumulate oil, rubber, and chemical residue that creates slip hazards and degrades the epoxy coating over time. Routine degreasing and auto-scrubbing is a liability management tool.

What we do: Industrial degreasing with agitation, auto-scrubbing for embedded contamination, oil & rubber residue extraction, epoxy inspection and repair, anti-slip treatment for hazard zones.

You receive: Hazard identification log, chemical application records, corrective action documentation, post-treatment slip-resistance readings, safety compliance file.

Quarterly · Safety compliance records

Hardwood & Sports Floors

Hardwood is unforgiving of incorrect care. Excess moisture, acidic cleaners, or abrasive equipment causes permanent cupping, warping, and finish damage. Our sports floor protocols follow manufacturer specifications — protecting both the floor and any applicable warranty.

What we do: Stone-safe daily dust mop. Moisture-controlled damp mop. Seasonal conditioning and screen-and-recoat. Sports floors: MFMA protocol, line marking protection.

You receive: Product log with manufacturer approval, moisture reading records, finish inspection report, before/after photographs, sports floor condition report.

Seasonal · Green cleaning compliant

Grout & Tile

Grout is porous. Without sealing, it absorbs bacteria, oils, and cleaning chemicals. Discolouration that looks cosmetic is often a hygiene issue. Restoration removes embedded contamination and reseals the surface, making routine cleaning effective again.

What we do: High-pressure hot-water grout cleaning, alkaline pre-treatment, steam sanitisation for healthcare/food areas, grout sealer with product spec logged.

You receive: Before/after condition photographs, chemical & dwell time log, sealer specification & application record. Hygiene documentation available for healthcare environments.

Annual · CDC Protocol

Frequently Asked Questions

What Facility Managers Ask Us

How do you handle buildings with multiple floor types?

Each surface type gets its own protocol — tile in restrooms, carpet in offices, concrete in loading areas — with appropriate products and equipment for each. No one-size-fits-all approach.

How often should commercial floors be deep cleaned?

It depends on traffic and floor type. A typical programme: daily maintenance, hot-water carpet extraction every 3–6 months, VCT strip and wax annually or semi-annually for high-traffic areas. We assess the specific facility and recommend accordingly.

What documentation is provided?

Complete documentation for every visit — cleaning logs, product certifications, quality scores, and compliance verification. All records available on demand and formatted for regulatory or audit presentations.

How do you manage compliance requirements?

Compliance requirements are embedded in your custom cleaning plan from day one. Our system tracks applicable requirements and integrates them automatically — without additional management overhead on your part.

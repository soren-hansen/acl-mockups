Frequently Asked Questions

How does Advantage Cleaning maintain consistent quality across multiple school campuses?

Every campus operates against a documented cleaning plan specific to that building's layout, traffic patterns, and compliance requirements. A dedicated supervisor inspects each shift against agreed KPIs. Weekly KPI reports cover all campuses in one document — your single account manager oversees every location and is the one person to call for any issue.

Does Advantage Cleaning provide green cleaning products for school contracts?

Green cleaning products are standard on every school contract. Supporting documentation is generated automatically and available for your compliance records — without you having to ask for it.

What documentation does Advantage Cleaning provide for school contracts?

Every contract includes weekly KPI reports, green cleaning compliance documentation, safety compliance records, and inspection records available on demand.

How does Advantage Cleaning handle additional school locations?

Adding new campuses is handled through the same systematic approach. A facility-specific assessment is conducted, a custom cleaning plan is built for that location, and it is integrated into your centralised oversight system. New campuses start to the same documented standard as existing locations from day one.

How does Advantage Cleaning work around the school calendar?

All work is scheduled to minimise disruption — cleaning after hours, floor work during breaks, and coordination with principals for access. Summer programmes are structured differently: the annual deep clean is typically completed June–August. All scheduling is agreed in the custom cleaning plan specific to each campus.

What is Advantage Cleaning's response time for outbreak situations?

Same-day response for declared outbreak situations. Electrostatic disinfection equipment and CDC-aligned protocols are maintained and ready for rapid deployment. Your account manager coordinates directly with your facilities team — one call triggers the response.

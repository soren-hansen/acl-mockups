FAQ

Questions Facility Managers Ask

What is a day porter service?

A day porter is a dedicated on-site cleaning technician who maintains your facility during business hours — lobbies, restrooms, common areas, spill response, consumable restocking. Unlike overnight janitorial, a day porter operates while your building is occupied, addressing real-time conditions as they arise throughout the day.

What is the difference between a day porter and a janitor?

A janitor typically works overnight when the building is empty, handling deep cleaning, floor care, and full restroom servicing. A day porter works during business hours, maintaining standards in real time as the building is occupied — responding to spills, restocking consumables, and keeping high-traffic areas presentable throughout the day. The two programmes work best together.

How is Advantage Cleaning’s programme different from a standard porter service?

Most providers send a porter and rely on the FM to manage quality. We operate a supervised programme: a named supervisor oversees task completion, every zone is scored on a 1–5 QA scale, tasks are logged in real time, and the FM receives a weekly report. The FM does not manage the porter. The system does.

Can day porter services be combined with an overnight janitorial programme?

Yes — and most of our clients run both. The overnight crew handles deep cleaning, floor care, and full restroom servicing. The day porter maintains standards during business hours. One programme, one supervisor, one QA system across both.

What documentation does the FM receive?

Weekly QA reports with zone-by-zone scores, task completion logs, issues flagged and resolved, and consumable usage summaries. Inspection records are available on demand.

What types of facilities use day porter services?

Office buildings, corporate HQs, hotels, retail and commercial spaces, museums and cultural institutions, schools, healthcare and medical offices, and multi-tenant commercial buildings. Any facility with high foot traffic during business hours benefits from a day porter programme.

What hours does a day porter work?

Shift length and timing are agreed based on your building’s occupancy schedule. Typical day porter shifts run 4–8 hours during peak business hours. We build the schedule around your facility — not a standard template.

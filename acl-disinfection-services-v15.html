FAQ

Questions Facility Managers Ask

What is the difference between cleaning and disinfection?

Cleaning removes visible dirt and debris. Disinfection uses EPA-registered chemical agents — applied at the correct concentration and left for a required dwell time — to eliminate pathogens on treated surfaces. The two are separate processes. Our disinfection service follows both steps, in sequence, with documentation after each job.

What technology do you use for disinfection?

Electrostatic spraying and hospital-grade EPA-registered disinfectants. Electrostatic sprayers charge disinfectant particles so they wrap around surfaces — including sides and undersides — giving consistent coverage across complex surfaces. For enclosed spaces, UVC light treatment is also available.

What documentation do you provide after a disinfection job?

A digital report with inspection scores for each treated zone, photos where applicable, and any follow-up recommendations. Inspection records are available on demand. If there’s a compliance review or health department inquiry, you have the records.

What does GBAC-certified mean?

GBAC (Global Biorisk Advisory Council) is an internationally recognised certification for cleaning professionals trained in biohazard remediation and pathogen management. Our staff hold this certification — it covers correct chemical usage, pathogen response protocols, and outbreak prevention procedures.

Do you handle emergency disinfection?

Yes. We respond to emergency situations including biohazard incidents, spills, and facility events requiring immediate remediation. Contact us directly at 718-701-6806.

What types of facilities do you disinfect?

Office buildings, schools and educational facilities, healthcare and medical facilities, industrial and warehouse facilities, post-construction projects, and retail and commercial spaces across New York City and the tri-state area.

How do you determine the right disinfection protocol for my facility?

We assess your facility before any work starts — surface types, traffic patterns, occupancy schedules, and specific risk areas. From that assessment, we produce a written disinfection plan. The protocol, product selection, and frequency are matched to your specific facility type.

Can disinfection be integrated with an ongoing janitorial programme?

Yes. Most of our commercial clients run disinfection as part of a broader facility maintenance programme — daily or nightly janitorial service, with scheduled disinfection cycles at a defined frequency. One programme, one point of contact, one quality system across all services.
